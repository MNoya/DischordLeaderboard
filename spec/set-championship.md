# The Set Championship

Every Magic set ends with a Set Championship: a tournament where the top-ranked members of the community draft against each other, and the winner earns the Set Champion role. It runs once per set. The leaderboard decides who plays. The eight highest-ranked players by standings take the seats.

This guide describes what happens each season, start to finish. It stays current as the system changes.

## When it happens

The championship runs on the Saturday before the next set's prerelease weekend, at 2 PM ET. So the format that is ending gets its send-off just as the new set is about to arrive. For MSH the date is Aug 1, 2 PM Eastern.

Everything below runs on its own. The bot posts the announcement, the invitations, and the seeding. No mod sets it up by hand.

## The week before

About five days out, the bot posts the championship card in the pod channel. The card announces that the set is closing, links the final standings and the stream, and states the rules. It opens a thread, the same as any pod signup.

At that moment the standings freeze, so the seed order is set from the leaderboard as it stands when the card posts.

## The invitations

Over the next few hours, the bot sends three invitation waves into the thread:

- The first invites the top 10 on the board.
- The second extends it to ranks 11 through 20.
- The third reaches ranks 21 through 32.

Each wave links back to the championship card and carries three buttons: **Confirm**, **Maybe**, **Can't**. Answering on a wave records the same thing as answering on the card.

The pings exist to make sure players see the event, not to gate it. Every wave goes out no matter how many players have already confirmed, and anyone may sign up whether or not they were pinged. A player who already answered is shown by status next to their name instead of being pinged again.

## The seeding

Once the invitations are out, the bot posts a seeding table in the thread. It lists everyone who confirmed, ranked by the leaderboard, with the top 8 seated and the rest below as alternates. The table updates in place as more players confirm, so it always shows the current picture.

## On the day

On championship Saturday the pod launcher's afternoon slot is not a normal joinable slot. It points to the championship instead. The evening Late Pod runs as usual, so regular drafting still happens that night.

Players sign up on the card if they can make it. Signing up early helps set the roster ahead of time. Seats go to the highest standings among those who show up. The rest are alternates for no-shows: a player who confirmed but is not present at event time is passed over for anyone on the wait list.

## Rules

- 8-player Pod Draft, Best-of-Three matches, Swiss Tournament, three rounds paired by record. Every player plays all three rounds.
- Player selection and seeding follow the leaderboard standings, combining the scores of all available queues of the latest format.
- Decklists are closed for all players.

## Logistics

- GatoDelFuego streams the event at [twitch.tv/gatodelfuego](https://www.twitch.tv/gatodelfuego). One match is featured on stream at a time.
- The draft runs on [draftmancer.com](https://draftmancer.com).
- Both players in a featured match screen share on Discord. Muting Discord audio is encouraged so viewers can join and leave without distraction.
- One player finishes on top and earns the Set Champion role.
